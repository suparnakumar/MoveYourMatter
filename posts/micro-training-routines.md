Here is a thing that is true and underappreciated: you can change your brain's state in minutes.

Not in the vague, motivational-poster sense. In the neurochemical, measurable, you-will-actually-feel-it sense. The key is doing the right thing — not more of the wrong thing, faster.

## Why Coffee Doesn't Work (Not Really)

Caffeine is the world's most popular cognitive enhancer. It works by blocking adenosine receptors — the receptors responsible for making you feel tired. What it doesn't do is change your brain's underlying state. It masks fatigue. It doesn't resolve it.

A 5-minute movement practice does something fundamentally different. It triggers the release of DOSE — Dopamine, Oxytocin, Serotonin, and Endorphin. These are not stimulants. They are the brain's own signals for motivation, connection, calm, and wellbeing. They shift your cognitive state at the source, not by suppressing the signals of exhaustion.

## What "The Right Thing" Looks Like

Not all movement resets the brain equally. Walking around the block is better than nothing. But it is not optimised for cognitive shift.

What works is movement that:

- **Requires active attention** — your brain must track what your body is doing
- **Involves rhythm** — rhythmic movement synchronises neural oscillations and promotes calm alertness
- **Coordinates multiple body parts** — bilateral, cross-body movement engages both hemispheres simultaneously
- **Includes a sequence to remember** — memory and motor systems working together accelerates the neurochemical response

This is the design logic behind MoveYourMatter's daily practice. Every 7-minute session hits all four criteria. That is why it feels different from a walk. You are not just moving. You are giving your brain a specific kind of input — one it is built to respond to.

## The Honest Part

A single session will not fix chronic burnout. It will not undo weeks of poor sleep or months of compounding stress. Anyone who tells you otherwise is selling something.

What a single session will do — consistently, from day one — is shift your state. You will finish the 7 minutes feeling different from when you started. That shift is real. It is biological. And it compounds over time.

Ninety days of daily practice produces neurological change that is measurable and lasting. But you will feel the first benefit today.

That is the honest version of "a 5-minute brain reset that actually works."
